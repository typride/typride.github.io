Obsidian

Productivity & Knowledge Management active ★ 4.8 freemium · $10/month Free tier available

Obsidian is a powerful and extensible knowledge base that works on top of a local folder of plain text Markdown files. It allows users to create a "second brain" by connecting notes through bidirectional linking. Its strength lies in its local-first philosophy, extensive plugin ecosystem, and graph view, which visualizes the connections between notes.

Key Features

Bidirectional LinkingCreate a network of your knowledge by linking notes to each other. See all the notes that link to the current note in the backlinks panel.
Graph ViewVisualize the connections between your notes as an interactive graph, helping you discover new relationships and insights.
Plugin EcosystemA vast library of community-built plugins that add a wide range of functionality, from calendars and Kanban boards to AI assistants.
Local-First & Plain TextAll your data is stored in Markdown files on your local machine, giving you full ownership and control.

Use Cases

  • Building a personal knowledge management (PKM) system
  • Academic and professional research and note-taking
  • Writing and organizing long-form content like books or theses

Pros

  • Your data is stored locally in plain text files, ensuring privacy and longevity
  • Massive plugin ecosystem allows for endless customization and new features
  • The graph view provides a unique way to visualize and discover connections
  • Completely free for personal use without feature limitations

Cons

  • Syncing between devices requires a paid subscription or a complex third-party setup
  • Can have a steep learning curve for beginners due to its extensive features
