Capacities

Productivity & Knowledge Management active ★ 4.5 freemium · $10/month Free tier available

Capacities is a personal knowledge management tool that uses object-based notes to help you organize your thoughts and ideas in a more natural and intuitive way. It moves beyond traditional hierarchical file structures, allowing you to create a network of interconnected notes that reflects the way your brain works.

Try Capacities →

Key Features

Object-Based NotesStructure your knowledge around objects like people, books, or ideas, rather than rigid folders.
Networked ThinkingConnect notes to create a graph of your knowledge, helping you to see the bigger picture and make new connections.
Daily NotesA dedicated space to capture your thoughts and activities each day, which can then be linked to your broader knowledge base.
Media IntegrationUpload images, PDFs, and other files directly into your notes.

Use Cases

  • Organize research and notes for academic or professional projects.
  • Build a personal knowledge base for lifelong learning.
  • Manage meeting notes, ideas, and project-related information.

Pros

  • Object-based note-taking provides a flexible and powerful way to organize information.
  • The networked structure helps in discovering connections between ideas.

Cons

  • The learning curve can be steeper compared to traditional note-taking apps.
  • Mobile app functionality might not be as robust as the desktop version.

Comparisons

While Notion is a powerful all-in-one workspace, Capacities focuses specifically on creating a personal knowledge graph.Capacities vs ObsidianCompared to Obsidian, Capacities is a cloud-based service with a more guided user experience, whereas Obsidian is a local-first, markdown-based tool that is highly customizable with plugins.
