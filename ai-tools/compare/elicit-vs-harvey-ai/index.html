Overview

Elicit

An AI research assistant for academic literature review. Searches and summarizes research papers, extracts key findings, and helps identify relevant studies.

Harvey AI

Harvey AI is an advanced AI platform specifically designed for legal and professional services. It excels in streamlining legal research, drafting various legal documents, and conducting thorough contract reviews. Pros & Cons

Elicit

Pros
  • Understands research methodology, not just keywords
  • Extracts structured data from papers (sample size, methods, findings)
  • Saves weeks of manual paper sifting for lit reviews
  • Good at surfacing relevant papers you wouldn't find via keyword search
  • Free tier is usable for small projects
Cons
  • Coverage gaps in some niche fields
  • Summaries can miss nuance in complex studies
  • Limited to academic papers (not patents, reports, etc.)
  • Plus plan needed for serious research volume

Harvey AI

Pros
  • Significantly accelerates legal research and drafting processes
  • Provides a secure platform for sensitive legal work
  • Ensures consistency in legal document generation
  • Integrates into existing legal workflows and day-to-day tools
Cons
  • High cost, making it potentially inaccessible for smaller firms or individual practitioners
  • May struggle with highly nuanced, jurisdiction-specific legal queries
  • Could be more user-friendly in some aspects

Use Cases

Elicit

  • Systematic literature review and paper discovery
  • Extracting key findings across multiple papers
  • Research question formulation and refinement
  • Identifying methodology patterns across studies
  • Building annotated bibliographies

Harvey AI

  • Streamlining contract reviews
  • Creating compliance materials
  • Generating client-ready work product
  • Accelerating time-consuming parts of litigation, such as depositions

Our Take

Elicit has a higher user rating (4.6 vs 4.0). Elicit offers a free tier, making it easier to try before you buy.
